# Three Mornings Before the World Wakes

![Morning light through a kitchen window](/images/IMG_0347.jpeg)

**First morning** — I wake at 5:47 without an alarm. The house is silent. I make coffee and do not check my phone. For thirty minutes, I just sit. The cat jumps on my lap. I watch the light change from grey to gold.

**Second morning** — I write three pages by hand. Nothing important. Just the weather, a dream I half‑remember, a worry that has been circling. The act of moving a pen across paper is enough. I do not need to publish it. I do not need to share it.

**Third morning** — I go for a walk before sunrise. The streets are empty except for a baker unloading bread and a dog walker yawning. I realise: the world does not need me to be productive at 6 AM. It just needs me to be *present*.

![A quiet street at dawn](/images/IMG_0349.jpeg)

Three mornings. No breakthroughs. No grand insights. Just the quiet proof that we do not have to earn the day — we only have to show up for it.
