# Golden Hour, Any Hour

![Warm afternoon light on a wooden floor](/images/IMG_0351.jpeg)

Photographers chase the golden hour — the twenty minutes after sunrise or before sunset when the light turns honey‑soft. But I have learned that golden light exists everywhere, at any hour, if you are willing to see it.

Yesterday at 2:17 PM, I was washing dishes. The sun came through the window at exactly the right angle and turned the soap bubbles into tiny prisms. For ten seconds, the kitchen was a cathedral.

![A glass of water catching the afternoon sun](/images/IMG_0347.jpeg)

We are told that beauty requires planning: a trip to the mountains, a museum, a concert. But most beauty is unannounced. It arrives when you are not looking — in the steam of tea, the curve of a friend’s smile, the way dust floats in a ray of light.

> Do not wait for golden hour. Make any hour golden by paying attention.

The image above is a glass of water on a Tuesday. Nothing special. But the light decided it was special. And for a moment, I agreed.
