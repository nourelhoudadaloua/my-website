# The Museum of Unfinished Memory

![A quiet room with light falling on an old photograph](/images/IMG_0347.jpeg)

We are all curators of a museum that never opens to the public. Inside: ticket stubs, dried flowers, voicemails we cannot delete, the smell of a grandmother’s kitchen. 

I have been thinking about what we choose to keep. Not the grand events — the wedding, the graduation — but the *almost* things. The letter never sent. The friendship that dissolved into silence.

> Memory is not a storage device. It is a living, breathing act of selection.

This photograph (above) was taken on a winter afternoon. The light fell exactly as it does in dreams: soft, forgiving, slightly out of focus. That is how most of our memories arrive — not as sharp facts, but as *impressions*.

**What do you keep in your museum?**  
Perhaps it is time to walk through the halls and simply notice what hangs on the walls.
