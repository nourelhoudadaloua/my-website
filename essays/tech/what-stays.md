# What Stays After the Fire

![A charred photograph frame with a barely visible image](/images/IMG_0352.jpeg)

Last year, a friend’s house burned down. She lost everything — except a small metal box under the sink. Inside: her grandmother’s ring, a USB drive of old photos, and a letter from her father written when she was ten.

She told me: “I realised I had been storing my memories in things that could vanish.”

> Grief is not about loss. It is about what refuses to leave.

The photograph above is not from her fire, but it could be. The image is nearly gone. The frame is blackened. Yet you can still see two figures — maybe holding hands, maybe dancing.

That is what stays. Not the object, but the echo. 

I have stopped worrying about preserving everything. Now I try to notice what already survives: a laugh that sounds exactly like someone you lost. A recipe made from memory. The way light falls on a familiar chair.

The fire takes the frame. But the dance remains.
